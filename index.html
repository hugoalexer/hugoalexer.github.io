My research focuses on applied optimization and statistical analysis, to develop models that drive data-informed decision-makin. I am particularly interested in machine learning, business analytics, and design of experiments, with a current focus on industrial and educational applications.

I began my academic career at the Tuxtla Gutierrez Institute of Technology (now TecNM). My journey includes over four years at Universidad Anáhuac México, where I received a Teaching Award in 2014. Currently, I am a Full-Time Professor at Universidad Iberoamericana within the Department of Chemical, Industrial, and Food Engineering.

I hold a PhD in Industrial Engineering from CADIT with a minor in optimization, an M.S. in Systems Engineering from UANL, and a B.S. in Industrial Engineering from TecNM). My technical expertise spans operations research, logistics, and advanced industrial engineering.
