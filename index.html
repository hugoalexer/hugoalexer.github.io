About me

I am a Full-time Professor in the Departamento de Ingeniería Química, Industrial y de Alimentos at Universidad Iberoamericana. I hold a Ph.D. in Industrial Engineering from Universidad Anáhuac México with a minor in optimization, an M.Sc. in Systems Engineering from UANL, and a B.S. in Industrial Engineering from Instituto Tecnológico de Tuxtla Gutiérrez.

My research lies at the intersection of applied optimization and statistical analysis. I develop mathematical models and computational frameworks to drive data-informed decision-making, with a particular emphasis on business analytics. Currently, I focus on the synergy between machine learning and industrial engineering to solve complex problems in logistics and educational environments, aiming to bridge the gap between theoretical modeling and practical applications.

I am a member of the Mexican National System of Researchers (SNII, Candidate level), a distinction that complements my international recognition as an Outstanding Professor (2025) by the IEOM Society International. My commitment to the intersection of research and teaching was further honored with the ANUIES Award for Educational Innovation (2024), reflecting a career dedicated to advancing industrial engineering through rigorous academic excellence and high-impact mentorship.
