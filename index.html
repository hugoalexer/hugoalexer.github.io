My doctoral studies were in Industrial Engineering with a minor in optimization. I hold a Master's degree in Systems Engineering. Additionally, I have extensive experience in Applied Statistics and Operations Research.

My research focuses on applied optimization and statistical learning, exploring both theoretical models and their algorithmic implications. I am particularly interested in machine learning, design of experiments, and business analytics. Currently, my work is centered on teaching and research-oriented industrial applications.

I began my academic career at the Tuxtla Gutierrez Institute of Technology (now TecNM). My journey includes over four years at Universidad Anáhuac México, where I received a Teaching Award in 2014. Currently, I am a Full-Time Professor at the Department of Chemical, Industrial, and Food Engineering at Universidad Iberoamericana.

I hold a PhD in Industrial Engineering with a focus on optimization, an M.S. in Systems Engineering from UANL, and a B.S. in Industrial Engineering from ITTG. My expertise spans applied statistics, operations research, and combinatorial optimization.
