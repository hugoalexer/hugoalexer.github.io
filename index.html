Bio

I am a Full-Time Professor in the Department of Chemical, Industrial, and Food Engineering at Universidad Iberoamericana. I hold a PhD in Industrial Engineering from CADIT with a minor in optimization, an M.S. in Systems Engineering from UANL, and a B.S. in Industrial Engineering from TecNM. My academic trajectory includes over four years at Universidad Anáhuac México and foundational years at the Tuxtla Gutierrez Institute of Technology.

My research lies at the intersection of applied optimization, and statistical analysis. I develop mathematical models and computational frameworks to drive data-informed decision-making, with a particular emphasis on business analytics. Currently, I focus on the synergy between machine learning and industrial engineering to solve complex problems in logistics and educational environments, aiming to bridge the gap between theoretical modeling and practical applications.

My work has been recognized through several distinctions. I am a member of the Mexican National System of Researchers (SNII, Candidate level). Recently, I was honored by the IEOM Society International in Sorocaba, Brazil (2025) as an Outstanding Professor in Applied Optimization and Statistics, and I had the privilege of mentoring the winners of the Undergraduate Student Paper Competition sponsored by Siemens. Additionally, my commitment to educational innovation was recognized with an award by ANUIES (2024), complementing my earlier Academic Excellence and Teaching awards from Universidad Anáhuac.
